Annotated Bibliography

Zemlin, Lucas. Spring 2021. Media and Communication Studies. Analysis of “The Social Dilemma”

This paper is a review of a Netflix documentary about social media and its pervasive and insidious effect on people’s opinions, views, and even the information that they can access. It details the big data industry behind Facebook, Google, and other large information and communication technology platforms which aggregate meta data about their users for profit driven purposes.

I loved writing this paper because it combined my interest in psychology and information technology. The film itself is disturbing to say the least but it does cover a very contemporary and important topic. I enjoyed summarizing and expressing my opinion about the questionable practices behind the “big data” industry that are addressed in the film. I was able to demonstrate knowledge I’ve gained in areas of psychology, information security, and media communication.

Zemlin, Lucas. Winter 2021. BES 301, BES 301 Final Paper.

This paper is a summarization of a scientific and statistical review of massive amounts of data points about a select few bird populations within Washington State. It compares population differences in these species across different areas of Washington.

While the topic of this paper was not particularly interesting to me personally. I choose to include this artifact because I enjoyed learning to use statistical analysis of data points to create hypothesizes and then prove them to be correct or incorrect mathematically. It was an important part of learning how to objectively analyze data. This is a relevant skill across a wide array of professions in the fields of science.
