About Me

Hello! 

My name is Lucas Zemlin. Describing oneself is always difficult, but I’ll try to be succinct. I grew up in Kirkland, WA. I graduated from Lake Washington High School in 2010 and pursued a career in music. After a few years I realized that making my music into a business was taking away from my enjoyment of playing. I made the decision to go back to college. You’ll read about all that later though!

As a person I like to remain active with hobbies. I still to this day compose and record music, both collaboratively and on my own. Among my other hobbies are hiking, web development, gaming, and reading. I’ve moved around, but I currently live in Kirkland, WA again with my partner Sara and our kitten Timon.

Music Career

My first instrument was the piano whereas a child I learned the basics. In Junior High I joined the concert and jazz bands playing the trombone. I started taking private guitar lessons around this time for I quickly found I preferred it to the trombone. When I left home, I moved to Portland, OR to study music and audio engineering at Portland Community College. During these years I played in various bands, ranging from live instrumentational hip hop to jazz improv. Monetarily my best gigs were with cover bands playing Motown music.

The music industry is an extremely difficult business to make a steady living. Forty years ago, before the age of the internet and streaming, a musician could make a living playing gigs and not have to ascend to rockstar status to pay the bills. Today the business has become much more of a grind, and as a result the idea of the middle-class musician has become less feasible, with most professional musicians having to work day-jobs to support themselves.

Today I play music for my enjoyment, if I ever make money from it then it’s all gravy. The music I compose now is often eclectic as are my tastes. Some of my songs are classical in nature, others are funk driven and contemporary. I love exploring electronic sounds using MIDI, it’s a powerful tool to leverage virtual studio technologies (VST) to create limitless ranges of synth tonalities.

Current College Trajectory

My current major in college is psychology, with a minor in computer science. I love psychology because the human mind is still to this day a mystery in many ways. Much like a blackhole, the mind is a powerful entity that we can only make inferences about based on observation. Many details of what lies inside, what creates our personal realities, our consciousness, remain hidden so far. For these reasons, cognitive psychology is by far my favorite subject in psychology.

After taking computer science classes at the UW for my minor, I realized that I not only really enjoyed it but also had some aptitude for it. As a result, I’m currently working toward a double major in Applied Computing and Psychology. I’m currently interning at PAC Worldwide in the Global Information and Systems Technologies branch, expanding my knowledge of network architecture, cloud computing, system administration, and cybersecurity.
