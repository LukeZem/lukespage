Zemlin, Lucas. Winter 2020. Physiological Psychology, Chains of Habit

This paper is an extensive review of scientific research papers surrounding opioid mechanisms of action, opioid addiction, and treatment. It first details the mechanisms of action and physiological psychology involved in opioid use and addiction. The end of the paper gives opinions of how and why opioid treatment has been largely ineffective until just recently, and potentially better solutions to a crisis. A solution suggested is to invest research into drugs that keep dopamine levels at normal ranges post-acute withdrawal and inhibit over production of NA.

This paper was both highly interesting for me to write and discusses a serious problem in the world. I chose this artifact because during my years as a professional musician I had many friends fall prey to opioid addiction. This paper allowed me to summarize and clearly articulate the mechanisms of opioid addiction and even suggest possible treatment options. It took me a significant amount time to review the surrounding literature and learn the physiological mechanisms of the brain to properly articulate the complex processes involved. Writing this paper also helped me get a solid understanding of the proper way to cite sources and paraphrase otherâ€™s words.

Zemlin, Lucas. Spring 2022. I/O Psychology, Pomegranate Bistro Project

This artifact is a presentation of the information gathered about a real-world business called Pomegranate Bistro. It includes an analysis of the business's practices and strategies from an I/O psychology point of view. The presentation also gives recommendations about areas for improvement. Such as creating better success incentives for employees, creating a more structured job proficiency appraisal system for new hires, and setting more clear key performance indicators and goals.

I chose this artifact because it represents an exercise of interacting with a real business and collaborating with other students to cohesively work as a team. We had to use concepts learned about industrial and organizational psychology and think about how we would apply them to the real world. We also used these same concepts and applied them to our work as a group. Since I/O psychology at its most basic level is really the study of teamwork, this project demonstrates knowledge that I can be highly valuable in literally any profession.

Zemlin, Lucas. Spring 2022. I/O Psychology, Peer Group Study

This paper is a reflection and analysis of a student group and how they worked together as a team. It uses I/O psychology concepts to analyze and suggest improvements in how they could operate.

This assignment was important to me because I got to observe another team over the course of a quarter and analyze how they worked together. At the end of the quarter the team provided private responses on how they thought they operated as a team. The skills learned in I/O psychology can be applied to any organization and it was helpful learning the value of these concepts by being able to closely observe a team in action.
